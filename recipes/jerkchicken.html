Jerk Chicken


Description

Jamaican Jerk Chicken - a well-seasoned grilled chicken with Jamaican jerk marinade that has the perfect balance of heat and sweetness. Amazingly tender, moist, and juicy chicken bursting with savory flavors on every bite. Youâ€™ll want to make this all year round!

Ingredients

For the Chicken Rub:

  • Whole Chicken
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Jerk Seasoning
  • Onion Powder
  • Garlic Powder

Jerk Marinade:

  • Sprig of Fresh Thyme
  • Scallions or Green Onion
  • Habanero Pepper
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Water or Pineapple Juice
  • Soy Sauce
  • Allspice
  • Brown Sugar
  • Cinnamon Powder
  • Nutmeg
  • Chicken Bouillon Powder

Steps

Season the Chicken

  1. Prep the chicken. Trim chicken of excess fat and pat dry with a cloth or paper napkin.
  2. Season and set aside. Rub with salt and freshly ground pepper, jerk seasoning, garlic powder, and onion powder. Set aside while you make the marinade.

Make the Jerk Marinade

  1. Pulse until blended. In a food processor or blender, combine the following: thyme, green onions, garlic, ginger, habanero pepper. Pulse for about 30 seconds until blended.
  2. Soak the chicken. Soak the chicken with a mixture of soy sauce, brown sugar, allspice, cinnamon, nutmeg, chicken bouillon powder, and water or pineapple juice, and then add the blended herbs and seasonings to complete the jerk marinade.
  3. Prep to refrigerate. Drench the chicken with jerk marinade, place in a zip lock bag and refrigerate for at least 3 hours, preferably overnight. Try not to touch the jerk blend with your hands because it is extremely spicy hot. If possible, use gloves.

Grill the Chicken

  1. Prep to grill. When ready to grill, remove chicken from the jerk marinade and place on medium to high lightly oiled grill. Reserve the remaining marinade.
  2. Start grilling. Cook chicken on the prepared grill for 12- 20 minutes, until juices run clear with an internal temperature of 165 degrees F, turning often to optimize cooking and browning. You may also broil or roast the chicken in a hot oven (425 degrees F).
  3. Simmer and serve. In a small saucepan simmer the remaining marinade and the one from the chicken for about 7 minutes. Serve with the chicken or mix it before serving with the plantains.
